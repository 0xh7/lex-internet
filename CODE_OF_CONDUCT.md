# Code of Conduct

Keep discussion respectful, direct, and technical. Disagreement is normal. Personal attacks are not.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful in technical disagreement
- Assume good faith and ask for clarification before escalating
- Keep feedback concrete, specific, and relevant to the change
- Help new contributors understand project constraints and expectations

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, abuse, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory or demeaning language
- Deliberate disruption of reviews, issues, or discussions
- Repeated bad-faith arguments after maintainers have made a decision
- Publishing private or security-sensitive details in public threads

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove content that breaks this code of conduct. Repeated or severe problems can lead to restricted participation.

## Reporting

If an interaction crosses the line, use a private maintainer or repository contact channel when one exists. If there is no private path yet, open a minimal issue asking for one and leave out sensitive details.
